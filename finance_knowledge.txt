Savings Rule:
Financial planners generally recommend saving at least 20 percent of monthly income to build long-term wealth and financial security.

Expense Rule:
If monthly expenses exceed 70 percent of income, the individual may face budgeting stress and limited flexibility for emergencies and investments.

Emergency Fund Rule:
A basic emergency fund should cover at least 3 to 6 months of essential living expenses to protect against job loss or medical emergencies.

High Expense Risk:
Spending more than 80 percent of income on monthly expenses increases the risk of debt and financial instability.

Low Savings Risk:
Saving less than 10 percent of income makes it difficult to achieve long-term financial goals such as buying a home or retirement planning.
Debt Rule:
If EMI payments exceed 30 percent of income, the user is at high debt risk.

Investment Rule:
Not investing any money leads to long-term wealth loss.

Impulse Spending Rule:
Frequent small purchases add up to major monthly expense leakage.

Rent Rule:
Housing costs should ideally not exceed 30 percent of income.

Savings Consistency Rule:
Irregular saving behavior creates financial instability.
